<objective>
MVP user story: As an external repo maintainer evaluating a poisoning-class caching tool, I want
a clear vulnerability-disclosure policy and an explicit license, so that I can adopt it under
known legal terms and know how to report a security issue responsibly (GOV-01, GOV-02).

This plan ships the two governance files a poisoning-class tool requires -- SECURITY.md (D-10)
and the MIT LICENSE (D-11) -- plus the maintainer-content email allowlist-inversion guard that
enforces the public-repo email hard rule (no work email/domain ever lands in a committed file).

Purpose: SECURITY.md is required for a CREEP/poisoning-class tool; the MIT LICENSE sets the
legal terms; the email guard makes the public-gmail-only rule a CI-enforced invariant rather
than a review-time hope.

Output: LICENSE (root), SECURITY.md, and packages/github-cache/src/governance-email.spec.ts.
</objective>
