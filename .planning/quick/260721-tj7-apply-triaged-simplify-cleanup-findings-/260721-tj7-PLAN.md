## Skipped findings (triaged out — do NOT implement)

- **resolveWriteIdentity extraction** (altitude #1 / reuse #2): consolidating the two bins'
  fail-closed identity preamble into a github-identity.ts composite breaks the deliberate
  intra-module mock seam — `action/index.spec.ts` overrides `resolveGitHubToken` on the module
  export, which would NOT intercept a composite's internal call. Security-invariant code; each
  bin already has its own fail-closed spec. Not worth fighting the test architecture.
- **server.ts switch -> ternary** (simplify #3): the `never` exhaustiveness guard is a deliberate
  compile-time tripwire for a future 3rd PutResult; a ternary drops that safety. Keep.
- **Shared 2 GiB constant** (reuse #5): `MAX_CACHE_BODY_BYTES` (HTTP body cap) and
  `RELEASE_ASSET_MAX_BYTES` (release-asset cap) are conceptually distinct limits that coincide
  today; a shared const would wrongly couple independent concerns.
- **publish-mirror.ts:257 redundant `mirrored === 0` conjunct** (simplify #4): harmless, and it
  documents the all-restore-MISS invariant explicitly. Not worth touching an observability guard.
- **All efficiency findings**: the sequential publish restore is intentionally serialized
  (per-entry ~2 GiB memory ceiling + Actions-cache rate limits); the per-get retention recompute
  is negligible and arguably deliberate (month rollover). No action.
